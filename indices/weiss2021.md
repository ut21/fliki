# Weiss, Goldberg, and Yahav — Thinking Like Transformers

**Proceedings of ICML**, 2021.

Introduces RASP (Restricted Access Sequence Processing), a programming language whose primitives—selectors and aggregation—correspond directly to transformer attention and feed-forward layers. Provides a practical framework for reasoning about what transformers compute.

## Core objects

- [RASP](../objects/rasp.md)

## Relation to other work

- The RASP framework was used implicitly or extended in several subsequent expressivity papers.
- [Strobl et al. 2024](strobl2024.md): the survey discusses RASP-like frameworks in the broader context of transformer expressivity.
